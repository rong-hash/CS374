Problem 2

Consider the following variants of the Towers of Hanoi. For each variant, describe an algorithm to solve it in as few moves as possible. Prove that your algorithm is correct.

  • Initially, all the nnn disks are on P1P_1P1​, and you need to move the disks to P2P_2P2​.
  • You are not allowed to put a bigger disk on top of a smaller disk.

(a) Hanoi1\text{Hanoi}_1Hanoi1​: You are forbidden to move any disk directly between P1P_1P1​ and P2P_2P2​. Exactly how many moves does your algorithm make as a function of nnn?

(b) Hanoi2\text{Hanoi}_2Hanoi2​: You are only allowed to move disks from P0P_0P0​ to P1P_1P1​, from P1P_1P1​ to P2P_2P2​, or from P2P_2P2​ to P0P_0P0​. Provide an upper bound, as tight as possible, on the number of moves that your algorithm uses.

(c) Hanoi3\text{Hanoi}_3Hanoi3​: The largest remaining disk disappears if there is nothing on top of it. The goal is to get all the disks to disappear. Again, provide a tight upper bound on the number of moves.
