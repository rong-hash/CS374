Solution

Both LIS recurrences are correct and yield identical results.

The first recurrence starts the candidate LIS A[1…i]A[1 \ldots i]A[1…i] from the beginning of the array and scans forward, selectively including numbers less than A[j]A[j]A[j]. LIS(i,j)LIS(i, j)LIS(i,j) represents the length of LIS in A[1…i]A[1 \ldots i]A[1…i] among numbers less than A[j]A[j]A[j].

On the contrary, the second recurrence starts from the end of the array and scans backward. LIS(i,j)LIS(i, j)LIS(i,j) represents the length of LIS in A[i…n]A[i \ldots n]A[i…n] among numbers greater than A[j]A[j]A[j].
