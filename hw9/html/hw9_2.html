Problem 2

Consider a decision problem XXX defined as follows:

  • Input: A directed graph G=(V,E)G = (V, E)G=(V,E) and a positive integer kkk
  • Output: True\text{True}True if there exists a subset V′⊆VV' \subseteq VV′⊆V with ∣V′∣≤k|V'| \le k∣V′∣≤k such that deleting the vertices in V′V'V′ and their adjacent edges from GGG leaves a DAG, False\text{False}False otherwise.

Show that XXX is NP-complete.


Solution

For an arbitrary undirected graph HHH, we construct a directed graph GGG by swapping every undirected edge (u,v)(u, v)(u,v) with two directed edges (u,v)(u, v)(u,v) and (v,u)(v, u)(v,u).

We reduce the decision problem XXX to the vertex cover problem and claim that X(G,k)=TrueX(G, k) = \text{True}X(G,k)=True iff H has a vertex cover of size at most kkk.

If. Suppose X(G,k)=TrueX(G, k) = \text{True}X(G,k)=True,

  • which means that we can delete at most kkk vertices from GGG to form a DAG.
  • Proof by contradiction. Suppose these deleted vertices does not form a vertex cover of HHH. Then the edges left out in HHH will "translate" into loops (u→v→uu \to v \to uu→v→u) in GGG, which is not a DAG.

Therefore, the deleted vertices of size at most kkk form a vertex cover of HHH.

Only if. Suppose HHH has a vertex cover V′V'V′ of size at most kkk.

  • Then deleting V′V'V′ and their adjacent edges from GGG leaves a number of isolated vertices, which collectively form a DAG.

Therefore, X(G,k)=TrueX(G, k) = \text{True}X(G,k)=True.  ■\ \blacksquare ■
