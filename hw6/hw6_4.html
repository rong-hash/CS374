Problem 4

Plum blossom poles are a Kung Fu training technique, consisting of nnn large posts partially sunk into the ground, with each pole pip_ipi​ at position (xi,yi)(x_i, y_i)(xi​,yi​). Students practice martial arts techniques by stepping from the top of one pole to the top of another pole. In order to keep balance, each step must be more than ddd meters but less than 2d2d2d meters.

Give an efficient algorithm to find a safe path from pole psp_sps​ to ptp_tpt​ if it exists.


Solution

Let the list of poles be P∈Rn×2P \in \R^{n \times 2}P∈Rn×2. We construct a graph G=(V,E)G = (V, E)G=(V,E) to describe the map, where V={p1,p2,…,pn}V = \{p_1, p_2, \dots, p_n\}V={p1​,p2​,…,pn​} are the poles and EEE is the set of all eligible moves.

FindSafePath(n,P,d,s,t)G←emptyGraph()sort P by xifor i←1 to nG.addVertex(pi)for i←1 to nfor j←i+1 to nif xj−xi≥2dbreakdist←(xi−xj)2+(yi−yj)2if dist∈(d,2d)G.addEdge(pi,pj)return DFS(G,s,t)\text{FindSafePath}(n, P, d, s, t) \\ \qquad G \gets \text{emptyGraph}() \\ \qquad \text{sort } P \text{ by } x_i \\ \qquad \textbf{for } i \gets 1 \text{ to } n \\ \qquad \qquad G.\text{addVertex}(p_i) \\ \qquad \textbf{for } i \gets 1 \text{ to } n \\ \qquad \qquad \textbf{for } j \gets i+1 \text{ to } n \\ \qquad \qquad \qquad \textbf{if } x_j - x_i \ge 2d \\ \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \textbf{break} \\ \qquad \qquad \qquad \text{dist} \gets \sqrt{(x_i - x_j)^2 + (y_i - y_j)^2} \\ \qquad \qquad \qquad \textbf{if } \text{dist} \in (d, 2d) \\ \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad G.\text{addEdge}(p_i, p_j) \\ \qquad \textbf{return } \text{DFS}(G, s, t) FindSafePath(n,P,d,s,t)G←emptyGraph()sort P by xi​for i←1 to nG.addVertex(pi​)for i←1 to nfor j←i+1 to nif xj​−xi​≥2dbreakdist←(xi​−xj​)2+(yi​−yj​)2​if dist∈(d,2d)G.addEdge(pi​,pj​)return DFS(G,s,t)

Time complexity.

  • We choose DFS because the map is 2D. We may expect the graph to be sparse and contain lots of "chains."
  • Constructing GGG takes O(n2)O(n^2)O(n2) time, but in practice this is a loose upper bound since the inner loop breaks early.
  • DFS takes O(n+m)O(n + m)O(n+m) time, where m=#Edges≤n2m = \text{\#Edges} \le n^2m=#Edges≤n2. Again, m≈nm \approx nm≈n in our sparse case.
  • Sorting PPP takes O(nlog⁡n)O(n \log n)O(nlogn) time. Total complexity is O(n2)O(n^2)O(n2) but can go as low as O(n)O(n)O(n) for sorted input and sparse graphs.
