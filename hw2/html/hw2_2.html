Problem 2

Let

Σ={[00],[01],[10],[11]} \Sigma = \{\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\} Σ={[00​],[01​],[10​],[11​]}

Consider each row to be a binary number and let

C={w∈Σ∗∣the bottom row of w is three times the top row}C = \{ w \in \Sigma^* \mid \textrm{the bottom row of } w \textrm{ is three times the top row} \} C={w∈Σ∗∣the bottom row of w is three times the top row}

Show that CCC is regular.


Solution

We construct a DFA that accepts CCC to show that the language is regular.

Intuition

Important patterns include:

  • Standalone 111

[01][11] \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} [01​][11​]

Rigid Formulation

Another way to think about the condition that "the bottom row is 3 times the top row" is that

Bottom=shiftLeft(Top,1)+Top\textrm{Bottom} = \textrm{shiftLeft}(\textrm{Top}, 1) + \textrm{Top} Bottom=shiftLeft(Top,1)+Top

  • The DFA reads the characters of www from right to left as input.

  • Each input pair can be viewed operands of a full adder.

    • First operand (AAA) = Digit in the top row
    • Second operand (BBB) = Digit in the last input's top row
    • Sum (SSS) = Digit in the bottom row

  • To judge if a string is in the language, we check if the sum SSS (bottom row) is valid under the sum of AAA, BBB and CinC_{in}Cin​.
